# Sales Chat Playbook

This playbook outlines how the sales team should handle conversations initiated via the Tawk.to live chat widget.

## Objective
The goal of the chat is to provide **payment guidance**, confirm **project scope**, ensure the client is selecting the **right package**, and move them towards the next step (invoicing and onboarding).

## Workflow Guidelines

1. **Acknowledge and Review Context**
   * Review the tags and attributes sent by the website. You will see information like:
     * `source_cta` (e.g. "Ask About This Package", "Talk to Sales About This Service")
     * `selected_package` / `selected_service`
     * `page_url` / `page_title`
   * Acknowledge the specific package or service the user is asking about.

2. **Confirm Scope and Timeline**
   * Ask clarifying questions about their timeline, platform (e.g., Kajabi, Canvas), and content volume.
   * If the scope is large or undefined, guide them to fill out the **24-48 Hour Review** form (`/book-free-audit/`) to get a full assessment before quoting.

3. **Provide Estimates, Not Final Quotes**
   * Use language like: "Starting prices for this package are..." or "Based on what you've shared, we estimate..."
   * Remind them that final pricing is confirmed only after reviewing the full scope.

4. **Payment and Next Steps**
   * Use this standard copy when discussing the project kick-off:
     > "A sales representative will help confirm the right package, scope, timeline, and payment next step before your project starts. After scope is confirmed, our team will share the appropriate invoice or payment instructions."
   * Do not claim "instant payment", "guaranteed payment", or "buy now".

5. **Avoid Over-Promising**
   * Do not guarantee instant replies (unless you are physically replying instantly).
   * Do not claim "lawyer-reviewed" terms or guarantees of specific results from previous case studies.

## Handling Common Queries
* **"How much does X cost?"** -> Provide the starting price and explain what factors influence the final quote (e.g., content volume, review rounds).
* **"Can you build what's in this case study?"** -> Yes, we can build a similar framework tailored to your needs. Let's discuss your specific audience and goals.
* **"I need help choosing a package."** -> Ask about their current stage (Idea, Content ready, Platform ready, Launching) to recommend the right fit.
